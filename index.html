Introduction

Who is KOMBIT?

KOMBIT is the IT project organization of the Danish municipalities. Founded to create cohesion and efficiency, KOMBIT purchases, manages, and develops IT solutions on behalf of the 98 municipalities in Denmark.

By centralizing IT procurement and management, KOMBIT ensures that critical infrastructure—serving millions of citizens—is secure, compliant, and cost-effective.

Core Infrastructure

Why ValueEdge (CSDP)?

To manage complex municipal IT projects, KOMBIT requires a robust testing foundation. Testing as a Service (TaaS) combined with OpenText ValueEdge delivers exactly that.

TaaS & CSDP Integration

Testing as a Service provides on-demand testing expertise and scalable resources. ValueEdge (the Core Software Delivery Platform) acts as the engine, providing a unified methodology across all of KOMBIT's testing initiatives.

Supplier Tech-Stack Integration

KOMBIT works with multiple external vendors. ValueEdge seamlessly integrates across diverse tech-stacks (Jira, Git, CI/CD tools), ensuring that no matter what the supplier uses, quality metrics are captured.

Combined Data in One Place

A single source of truth. By pulling data from automation scripts, manual test runs, and pipeline metrics, ValueEdge gives KOMBIT's leadership real-time visibility into release readiness and risk.

The Future

Using OpenText Aviator AI

Generative AI is transforming how we approach software testing. At KOMBIT, the adoption of Aviator AI represents the shift from manual test authoring to intelligent, automated generation.

Phase 1: Successful POC

The journey began with a Proof of Concept to validate Aviator's capabilities. We tested its ability to automatically generate manual test cases, BDD, User Stories from raw description, images and text and optimized existing work entities.

Phase 2: Now in Production

Moving beyond the POC, Aviator AI is now integrated into the production workflow. Test Managers leverage it to accelerate test design, summarize defect trends, and gain AI-driven insights into the overall risk profile of municipal software releases.
